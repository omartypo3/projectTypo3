tours and camps

Our Eintracht Frankfurt Football Academy specializes in grassroots football education for children and teens. Furthermore we promote a healthy lifestyle for children and the importance of sports to achieve their goals. Our Football Academy runs more than 35 camps every year. Most of them in Germany but also on international grounds like China, Abu Dhabi, USA and South Africa. Many of our Academy coaches are former professional players of Eintracht Frankfurt and all are well educated football coaches. Head of the Academy is our German football legend Karl-Heinz ”Charly“ Körbel, who holds the national record with most appearances in the German Bundesliga – all 602 caps for Eintracht Frankfurt.

YOUTH ACADEMY DEVELOPMENT

The Eintracht Frankfurt Youth Academy is located at the historic Riederwald site where Eintracht initially played their matches. Eintracht’s Youth Academy is continuously rated three stars – the highest potential standard for a Bundesliga club.

The philosophy of the Academy is to shape children from an early stage and develop them into professional football players. The current first team squad includes eight players that have gone through our youth development program. Several other players from our youth center now play professional football at other clubs in Germany or abroad.

The Academy has eight teams from Under-11 to Under-19 with a total of 160 players. The site provides four football pitches, an indoor pitch, a gymnasium, a medical center and housing for players.

Youth Development

The core value proposition of the club embraces “German Engineering of Football” in countries where the infrastructure and concept of playing football is not yet developed to its full potential. Establishing a sturdy foundation of youth football should ultimately result in better performance on the professional level, as well as simultaneously promoting an active lifestyle and equally-important values of team work, discipline and healthy competition.

Business Networks

Eintracht aims to build bridges between the business, finance and logistical sectors of the Frankfurt Rhine-Main with other global hubs that share an interest for economic cooperation beyond borders, such as Beijing, Tokyo and New York. Here, football works as a unique element to set the stage for people to unite and exchange thoughts in a friendly environment, overlooking current political tensions and disputes between nations. Eintracht recognizes the cultural differences that exist across the globe and the subsequent necessity for people to continue dialogue on a personal level.
